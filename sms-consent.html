SMS Consent & Opt-In

How LOFO collects consent before sending any SMS messages, what messages we send, and how to opt out.

How users opt in

LOFO is a lost-and-found matching app available on iOS and the web. Users opt in to receive SMS messages through a clear, optional checkbox displayed alongside the phone number input. The SMS consent checkbox is not pre-selected and is not required to submit the form — users can enter a phone number and proceed without checking it.

There are two screens where a phone number is collected, each with two separate checkboxes:

  • Finder phone verification screen — After a user photographs a found item, they are asked to enter their phone number. Two opt-in checkboxes are displayed alongside the phone input:

    Checkbox 1 (SMS consent — optional, not pre-checked): "By checking, you are allowing to receive transactional/informational SMS communications regarding item match notifications, pickup coordination, and account updates from LOFO. Messages frequency may vary. Message and data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help or STOP to opt out."

    Checkbox 2 (Terms — optional, not pre-checked): "By checking, I accept Terms of Service & Privacy Policy." (with tappable links to each document)
  • Owner phone notification screen — After a user describes a lost item and no immediate match is found, they can optionally enter their phone number to be notified when a match appears. The same two checkboxes with identical consent language are displayed alongside the phone input.

Opt-in methods include the LOFO iOS app and the LOFO web app (lofoapp.com). Both platforms display the same checkbox-based consent flow. Opt-in information is not shared with any third parties.

In both cases, the user must actively tap a button ("Send Code" or "Confirm Phone Number") to submit their number. The consent checkboxes and disclosure text are visible on-screen alongside the phone input. Checking the SMS consent box is not required to submit the form. "Terms of Service" and "Privacy Policy" are tappable links leading to the full Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

Consent is not a condition of using the app.

What messages we send

LOFO sends only transactional/informational SMS messages. We never send marketing, promotional, or advertising messages. The consent checkbox explicitly states "transactional/informational SMS." Message types include:

  • OTP verification codes — A one-time 6-digit code to verify the user's phone number (e.g., "Your LOFO verification code is 847291. Valid for 10 minutes.")
  • Match notifications — An alert when a potential match is found between a lost and found item (e.g., "Good news! Somebody found your lost item. Open the LOFO app to verify ownership and arrange return. Reply STOP to opt out.")
  • Coordination messages — Relayed messages between the finder and owner during the return process, sent through LOFO's number so neither party sees the other's real phone number
  • Lifecycle reminders — Occasional reminders about an active listing, such as a weekly encouragement message or an expiration notice at day 28

Message frequency

Message frequency varies based on user activity. A typical item submission generates 1–5 messages total (1 OTP code + 1 match notification + 1–3 coordination messages). Lifecycle reminders add at most 2 additional messages over a 30-day listing period. LOFO does not send recurring or scheduled marketing messages.

Opt-out: Reply STOP to any LOFO message to unsubscribe. You will receive a single confirmation and no further messages.

Help: Reply HELP to any LOFO message, or email support@lofoapp.com.

Costs: Message and data rates may apply. Contact your carrier for details.

Third-party sharing

Phone numbers collected through the LOFO app are not sold, rented, or shared with any third party for marketing purposes. Phone numbers are shared only with Twilio, our SMS delivery provider, solely for the purpose of delivering the messages described above.
